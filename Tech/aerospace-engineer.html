Aerospace Engineer

Description

Aerospace Engineers design, develop, test, and supervise the manufacturing of aircraft, spacecraft, satellites, and missiles. They work on aerodynamics, propulsion systems, avionics, and materials to improve flight technology.

Salary Range (India)

  • Entry-Level: ₹4 – ₹8 LPA
  • Mid-Level: ₹8 – ₹15 LPA
  • Senior-Level: ₹15 – ₹30+ LPA

Skills Required

  • Strong knowledge of aerodynamics, thermodynamics, and propulsion
  • Proficiency in CAD tools and simulation software (ANSYS, CATIA)
  • Problem-solving and analytical skills
  • Understanding of control systems and materials science
  • Teamwork and project management abilities

Future Aspects

With the rise of private space exploration, UAVs, and advanced aviation technology, aerospace engineering offers exciting career growth. Emerging areas include electric aircraft, space tourism, and hypersonic travel.

Top to Mid-Tier Companies That Recruit Aerospace Engineers

  • Top-Tier: ISRO, DRDO, HAL, Boeing, Airbus
  • Mid-Tier: L&T Aerospace, Honeywell, GE Aviation, Tata Advanced Systems
  • Others: Space startups, defense contractors, research labs

Famous Aerospace Engineers

  • Wernher von Braun – Pioneer in rocket technology and space exploration
  • Amelia Earhart – Aviation pioneer and female aviator
  • Kelly Johnson – Designed the Lockheed U-2 and SR-71 Blackbird
  • Satish Dhawan – Indian aerospace engineer and ISRO chairman
